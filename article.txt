                                     I. INTRODUCTION

For the electronic reliability, the failure and life of the

electrical solder materials such as Sn-Pb or Sn-Ag-Cu (SAC)

play the important roles so that the optimal design and accurate

failure prediction must be improved. Under the electric current

loads, the atomic migration phenomenon associated with voids

and failure is usually investigated in the electrical solder

material as shown in Fig. 1. There are four types of the atomic

migration phenomena: the electromigration, thermo-migration,

stress-migration and pure atomic diffusion [1-9]. The

governing equations are listed as follows [1-5]:

creation of void or hillock:

C < Cvoid : void formation       (9a)

C > Chill : hillock formation    (9b)                              Fig. 7. Electric current density (Jy, units: pA/m2) at t=16354000 s.

where Cvoid and Chill are the threshold values for the void and
hillock formation, respectively. According to ref. [1], Cvoid=
0.85 is adopted in this study. In addition, the hillock formation
is not considered and discussed.

    The void formation and growth process are simulated by
EBD technique in ANSYS [5]. For example, the method is
shown in Fig. 6. At t=t1, the whole field of the normalized
atomic density C is defined. In one element, if the region of
C<Cvoid exceed half element volume, this "death" element will
be removed so as to be a void formation at t=t2. Repeating the
process in Fig. 6, the void growth history can be obtained by
ANSYS.
